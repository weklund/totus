New Platform Totus Gives Users Full Ownership of Their Health Data — and Makes Sharing with Doctors Effortless

For millions tracking sleep, heart rate, glucose, and weight across different devices, the data is scattered and sharing it with doctors is a nightmare of screenshots and logins. Today, that changes.

Totus launches as the first web platform built for people who take their health seriously. It automatically pulls data from Oura Ring (and instantly from CGM monitors and smart scales via Apple Health and Google Fit) into one clean, interactive dashboard. Users can finally see years of trends, overlay sleep score with average glucose and weight, and spot patterns they’ve never noticed before.

But the real breakthrough is how Totus handles sharing. With a few clicks, users select exactly which metrics to share (e.g., last 90 days of sleep + glucose + weight), set an expiration date, and generate a private link. Doctors or coaches open the link in any browser — no account, no app, no hassle — and see beautiful charts. The link auto-expires, can be revoked instantly, and every view is logged in the user’s personal audit log.

“I used to email spreadsheets or print Oura reports for my doctor,” said early user Alex Rivera. “With Totus I sent one link that expired after my appointment. My doctor loved the clean visuals, and I loved knowing exactly who saw what and when.”

Privacy and control are baked in from day one. Data is encrypted with the same AWS standards hospitals use. Totus never sells data, never shows ads, and gives users a full activity log so nothing happens behind their back.

“Totus is your personal health vault,” said founder Wes Eklund. “You own the keys. You decide who gets a peek inside — and for how long. We built it because we were tired of fragmented apps and insecure sharing”

Totus is available today at totus.com as a free web platform while the team gathers feedback. Oura Ring support is live now; Apple Health and Google Fit integrations roll out in the next two weeks.

Totus is the simple, secure hub the quantified-self and metabolic-health communities have been waiting for — finally giving users true ownership and making doctor conversations faster and more useful.

## FAQ

### What is Totus?

Totus is a web-based personal health data vault that unifies your biometric data from Oura Ring, CGM monitors, smart scales, and more into one beautiful dashboard. It lets you explore long-term trends and securely share exactly the right slice with your doctor or coach — without giving away full access.

### How does Totus differ from Apple Health, Oura’s app, or other aggregators?

Apple Health and Oura are great at collection but terrible at clean long-term visualization and doctor-friendly sharing. Most aggregators either focus on insights (not control) or require everyone to create accounts. Totus is device-agnostic, web-first, and built around your ownership: granular, time-limited links + full audit transparency. No one else gives you a private viewer page that auto-expires and logs every view.

### What data sources are supported at launch?

MVP: Full Oura Ring integration.
v1.1 (two weeks after launch): Apple Health + Google Fit / Health Connect — this instantly brings in Dexcom Stelo, Freestyle Libre, Withings scales, and most other popular devices.

### How does the sharing feature work?

Click “Share”
Pick metrics + date range + expiration
Add optional note (“For Dr. Patel”)
Copy the private link
Recipient opens it in any browser — clean charts only. You see every view in your audit log and can revoke anytime.

### Is my data secure and private?

Yes. All data is encrypted at rest with AWS KMS (per-user keys), stored with hospital-grade controls, and never sold or used for ads. Only you can see your full dashboard. Share links are scoped and revocable. Full activity audit log included.

### Do doctors need a Totus account?

No. The link works for anyone with a web browser. Perfect for 10-minute appointments.

### Is Totus HIPAA compliant?

As a consumer-owned platform where you control sharing, Totus is not a covered entity. This keeps compliance simple for you and your doctor (you voluntarily disclose the data). We follow GDPR principles and bank-level security practices.

### How much does it cost?

Completely free during MVP while we gather feedback and add features.
